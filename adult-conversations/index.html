Adult conversations

I was recently doomscrolling Pinterest (yeah I know) and kept coming across screenshots from Tumblr/Twitter about parenting and people whose parents had a healthy "get me out of trouble" code. Okay, cool Fun Fact Nicole, what does this have to do with anything?

There's still more setup! I'm subscribed to Harvard Business Review's "management tip of the day" mailing list, and today's tip was about leading through uncertainty. The gist was essentially don't rush to a solution, don't assume you know everything, and realize that it's going to be a journey to get to the right answer/weather the storm/etc.

My epiphany: Isn't handling uncertainty essentially what an "adult conversation" is, where you bring up a difficult topic that does not have an immediately clear solution and you have to trust the other person to work with you on getting through whatever it is that needs to be talked about? I posit that this is the essence of what makes a conversation an "adult" conversation because it expects the best, most mature response from the conversationalists.

Given this definition of an adult conversation, the next logical question is on how to set up the conversation in such a way that the other person knows what the hell you're talking about. So how do you clearly say, "I don't know what the answer is and I trust you to work with me on a solution and not judge me for not knowing, and in response I will not judge you"? Or, "I do not want you to make me feel better, I want to address the issue even though it's going to be painful"? I have some ideas on ways to practice:

Practice within a team: say "I want to have an adult conversation about this topic because I don't know what to do here"

Practice with your partner: say "This thing is bothering me, I want to have an adult conversation about how both our needs can be met."

Practice with your child (if applicable): "I want to have an adult conversation with you about [topic]. The thing about adult conversations is that I don't already know the right answer - that's the thing about adult conversations, you have them even though it's scary to not know the answer, but you know you need to have the conversation. So I want to have an adult conversation with you so we can come up with an answer together, and I realize we might need to have this conversation several times before we get it right. [Explain the issue - child is misbehaving, burning out on school, not doing chores, whatever.]"

Try it out and let me know what you think! Or keep your opinion to yourself, it's all the same to me; I'll be over here looking at vintage sewing patterns on Pinterest.
