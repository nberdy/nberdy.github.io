On fitness

I started lifting weights again now that I live within walking distance of a gym. As such, I've been watching YouTube videos about proper lifting form and I joined a Facebook community of women who lift. Lifting communities tend to be segregated by gender, or at least the content creators tend to tailor their content to one gender over the other. As a result, the ads that track me all over the internet are full of products designed to either bulk me up (if I've been watching e.g. Built By Science on YouTube) or slim me down (e.g. Chloe Ting). (Do not judge my YouTube habits and I will not judge yours.)

Exercise is marketed very differently towards men vs women. For women it's all about slimming down, toning, losing weight, becoming SMALLER. For men though, it's all about bulking up, getting SWOLE, getting wider, becoming BIGGER. This really pisses me off! Men already tend to be more successful (wage gap), taking up extra space on the subway (manspreading) and taking up extra time in conversation (mansplaining). It's no wonder this happens when they're getting the message that they need to do MORE be MORE lift MORE.

Ladies, we need to get comfortable TAKING UP SPACE. Do our power poses, show up to that meeting early to get a literal seat at the actual table, play hallway chicken and DO NOT move out of the way for ANYONE. You have my permission to take up as much space as you do + 20% more than that, because we women tend to shrink down smaller than we actually need to without anyone saying a word. This is bullshit and needs to stop. You deserve that space. Nobody will give it to you, take that space, own it, get swole, own your muscles, I lost the plot with this but you get my point.

And remember to engage your core.
